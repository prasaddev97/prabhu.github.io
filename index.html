Dr. Prabhu Prasad Dev

Assistant Professor, School of Computer Engineering, KIIT Bhubaneswar
Post-Ph.D., National Institute of Technology (NIT) Calicut

I am an Assistant Professor at the School of Computer Engineering, KIIT Deemed to be University, Bhubaneswar, Odisha, India (QS Asia 2026: Rank 294 | NIRF 2025: Rank 27). My research focuses on Video Anomaly Detection, Person Re-Identification, and Biomedical Image Classification using computer vision and deep learning.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from the National Institute of Technology (NIT) Calicut, Kerala, India in November 2025, supervised by Dr. Pranesh Das and Dr. Raju Hazari. My thesis addressed Anomaly Detection in Surveillance Videos using Weakly Supervised Deep Learning Techniques.

My research has been published in top-tier venues including IEEE Transactions on Circuits Systems and Video Technology (IF: 11.1), Neurocomputing (IF: 6.5), Biomedical Signal Processing and Control (IF: 4.9), and the International Conference on Pattern Recognition (ICPR 2025, CORE B). I serve as a peer reviewer for Applied Soft Computing, Neurocomputing, and Pattern Recognition. I am an IEEE Member (#100903729), and have qualified UGC NET 2021 and GATE 2019.

Research Interests: Video Anomaly Detection  ·  Person Re-Identification  ·  Biomedical Image Classification  ·  Computer Vision  ·  Machine Learning  ·  Deep Learning  ·  Vision-Language Models  ·  Weakly Supervised Learning

Selected Publications

  1. 🎥
    Vision-Language · VAD
    ReFLIP-VAD: Towards Weakly Supervised Video Anomaly Detection via Vision-Language Model
    Prabhu Prasad Dev, Raju Hazari, and Pranesh Das
    IEEE Transactions on Circuits Systems and Video Technology, 2024
    IEEE T-CSVT · Q1 SCIE IF: 11.1
    DOI
    ReFLIP-VAD is a weakly supervised video anomaly detection framework that leverages vision-language model alignment to improve anomaly localization without requiring frame-level annotations, achieving state-of-the-art performance on benchmark surveillance datasets.
  2. 🧠
    Cross-Modal · Anomaly
    Cross-Modal Uncertainty Modeling Framework for Unseen Anomaly Detection
    Prabhu Prasad Dev, Pranesh Das, and Raju Hazari
    Neurocomputing, Elsevier, 2026
    Neurocomputing · Q1 SCIE IF: 6.5
    DOI
    A cross-modal uncertainty modeling framework that enables detection of anomalies not seen during training. By jointly reasoning over visual and semantic uncertainty signals, the framework generalizes to open-world anomaly scenarios in surveillance videos.
  3. 📹
    LLM · Multimodal · VAD
    MCANet: Multimodal Caption Aware Training-Free Video Anomaly Detection via Large Language Model
    Prabhu Prasad Dev, Raju Hazari, and Pranesh Das
    In International Conference on Pattern Recognition (ICPR), pp. 362–379, 2025
    ICPR 2025 · CORE B
    DOI
    MCANet introduces a training-free video anomaly detection pipeline by leveraging large language models to generate multimodal captions. Textual descriptions of video content are used as auxiliary signals to guide anomaly scoring without any task-specific fine-tuning.
  4. 🏥
    Biomedical · Parkinson's
    A modified Gray Wolf Optimization algorithm for early detection of Parkinson's Disease
    Krishnapriya Santhosh, Prabhu Prasad Dev, Binu Jose A, Zorana Lynton, Pranesh Das, and Ebrahim Ghaderpour
    Biomedical Signal Processing and Control, Elsevier, 2025
    BSPC · Q1 SCIE IF: 4.9
    DOI
    A modified Gray Wolf Optimization (GWO) algorithm applied to feature selection for early-stage Parkinson's Disease detection from biomedical signals, achieving improved classification accuracy over standard optimization baselines.
  5. 📝
    AI Text Detection · NLP
    Detecting AI-generated essays using fine-tuned XLNet-CNN hybrid techniques: a study of the academic integrity challenge
    Manish Prajapati, Santos Kumar Baliarsingh, and Prabhu Prasad Dev
    International Journal of Machine Learning and Cybernetics, Springer, 2026
    IJMLC · Springer · Q1 SCIE IF: 2.7
    DOI
    An XLNet-CNN hybrid model fine-tuned for detecting AI-generated academic essays, addressing growing challenges in maintaining academic integrity in an era of large language model proliferation.
  6. 🔍
    Multi-Stream · Deep Learning
    Msdeepnet: A Novel Multi-Stream Deep Neural Network for Real-World Anomaly Detection in Surveillance Videos
    Prabhu Prasad Dev, Pranesh Das, and Raju Hazari
    In International Conference on Deep Learning Theory and Applications, Springer, pp. 157–172, 2023
    DeLTA 2023 · Rome, Italy
    DOI
    Msdeepnet proposes a multi-stream deep neural architecture that processes complementary video features in parallel streams for robust real-world anomaly detection under varied surveillance conditions.
