Dr. Prabhu Prasad Dev

Post-Ph.D. · National Institute of Technology (NIT) Calicut  ·  IEEE Member

I am an Assistant Professor at the School of Computer Engineering, KIIT Deemed to be University, Bhubaneswar (QS Asia 2026: Rank 294 | NIRF 2025: Rank 27). My research focuses on Video Anomaly Detection, Person Re-Identification, and Biomedical Image Classification using computer vision and deep learning.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from NIT Calicut in November 2025, supervised by Dr. Pranesh Das and Dr. Raju Hazari. My thesis addressed Anomaly Detection in Surveillance Videos using Weakly Supervised Deep Learning Techniques.

My research appears in top venues including IEEE T-CSVT (IF: 11.1), Neurocomputing (IF: 6.5), BSPC (IF: 4.9), and ICPR 2025 (CORE B). I also serve as peer reviewer for Applied Soft Computing, Neurocomputing, and Pattern Recognition.

Selected Publications

  1. 🎥
    Vision-Language · VAD
    ReFLIP-VAD: Towards Weakly Supervised Video Anomaly Detection via Vision-Language Model
    Prabhu Prasad Dev, Raju Hazari, and Pranesh Das
    IEEE Transactions on Circuits Systems and Video Technology, 2024
    IEEE T-CSVT · Q1 SCIE IF: 11.1
    DOI ↗
    ReFLIP-VAD is a weakly supervised video anomaly detection framework that leverages vision-language model alignment to improve anomaly localization without requiring frame-level annotations, achieving state-of-the-art performance on benchmark surveillance datasets.
  2. 🧠
    Cross-Modal · Anomaly
    Cross-Modal Uncertainty Modeling Framework for Unseen Anomaly Detection
    Prabhu Prasad Dev, Pranesh Das, and Raju Hazari
    Neurocomputing, Elsevier, 2026
    Neurocomputing · Q1 SCIE IF: 6.5
    DOI ↗
    A cross-modal uncertainty modeling framework that enables detection of anomalies not seen during training. By jointly reasoning over visual and semantic uncertainty signals, the framework generalizes to open-world anomaly scenarios in surveillance videos.
  3. 📹
    LLM · Multimodal · VAD
    MCANet: Multimodal Caption Aware Training-Free Video Anomaly Detection via Large Language Model
    Prabhu Prasad Dev, Raju Hazari, and Pranesh Das
    In International Conference on Pattern Recognition (ICPR), pp. 362–379, 2025
    ICPR 2025 · CORE B
    DOI ↗
    MCANet introduces a training-free video anomaly detection pipeline by leveraging large language models to generate multimodal captions. Textual descriptions of video content guide anomaly scoring without any task-specific fine-tuning.
  4. 🏥
    Biomedical · Parkinson's
    A modified Gray Wolf Optimization algorithm for early detection of Parkinson's Disease
    Krishnapriya Santhosh, Prabhu Prasad Dev, Binu Jose A, et al.
    Biomedical Signal Processing and Control, Elsevier, 2025
    BSPC · Q1 SCIE IF: 4.9
    DOI ↗
    A modified Gray Wolf Optimization (GWO) algorithm applied to feature selection for early-stage Parkinson's Disease detection from biomedical signals, achieving improved classification accuracy over standard optimization baselines.
  5. 📝
    AI Text · NLP
    Detecting AI-generated essays using fine-tuned XLNet-CNN hybrid techniques
    Manish Prajapati, Santos Kumar Baliarsingh, and Prabhu Prasad Dev
    International Journal of Machine Learning and Cybernetics, Springer, 2026
    IJMLC · Springer · Q1 SCIE IF: 2.7

    An XLNet-CNN hybrid model fine-tuned for detecting AI-generated academic essays, addressing growing challenges in maintaining academic integrity in an era of large language model proliferation.
  6. 🔍
    Multi-Stream · DL
    Msdeepnet: A Novel Multi-Stream Deep Neural Network for Real-World Anomaly Detection in Surveillance Videos
    Prabhu Prasad Dev, Pranesh Das, and Raju Hazari

    DeLTA 2023 · Rome, Italy
